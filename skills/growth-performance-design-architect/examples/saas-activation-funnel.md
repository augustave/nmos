# SaaS Activation Funnel Optimization

## Scenario

A portfolio company's freemium-to-paid conversion is 2.1% against a 4% target.
Drop-off concentrates between signup and first-value-moment (creating a project).
The hypothesis: the onboarding flow has too many steps before the user sees output.

## Input

- Growth hypothesis: reducing onboarding from 6 steps to 2 will lift activation by 40%
- Funnel stage: activation
- Performance data: 12k signups/month, 68% complete step 1, 31% reach step 4, 22% create project
- Offer: free tier with 3 projects, upgrade at project limit
- Brand constraints: professional tone, no urgency language, no dark patterns
- Compliance: no implied savings claims without footnote

## Output

- Experiment design: A/B test — current 6-step vs. streamlined 2-step with deferred profile
- Landing page variant with message-match from top-performing ad creative
- Lifecycle email sequence (3 emails over 7 days) for users who stall
- Analytics instrumentation plan: 8 events tracking each micro-conversion

## Review Notes

The streamlined flow must not sacrifice data collection needed for personalization.
Defer profile fields to post-activation, but ensure the product can still function.
